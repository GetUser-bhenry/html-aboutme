KOREAN CULTURE
Korea is a culturally rich and fun country to explore. Whether you are interested in nature, temples, shopping, or clubbing, there is something in Korea for you. The most important things to keep in mind are that Korea has a drinking culture and that the custom courtesy is to respect elders. Everyone drinks- the legal drinking age is 19 in Korea, but they never check IDs. Also, you will probably experience elderly Koreans cutting you in lines or expecting you to move and that is because they treat their elders with a lot of respect in Korean culture. Many Koreans are active, even elders, and hike in the mountainous terrain outside of the major cities. Since you will most likely not have a car for the short Korea tour, you will rely on cab drivers. Be sure to annunciate yourself clearly and wear your seatbelt because they drive extremely fast and only avoid tickets because of monitors in their car that tell them where the speed checks are.
SEOUL
Seoul is a gigantic city which can’t even compare to New York because of the way that businesses are stacked on top of and beside each other. It is very busy, so be prepared for crowds at all times of the day and night. To get to Seoul you will need to take a 50 minute train ride to Seoul station and a cab to whichever area you would like to explore. Myeongdong is great for shopping and street food. Gangnam is the luxurious area which is also known for shopping. Hongdae is located near a university and has a lot of foreigners, so you will not feel uncomfortable there as an American and the young, college atmosphere is a plus!

LOCAL ACTIVITIES
While stationed in Korea you will most likely be at Camp Humphreys. Outside of Camp Humphreys is known as the “Ville.” They have all food options, from fried chicken to Korean pizza to teriyaki chicken on a stick! At night is when the clubs open! About 25 minutes away is Osan Air Base, which is where many go to club locally. They also have a “Ville” similar to the one outside of Camp Humphreys.
